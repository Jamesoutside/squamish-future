02
Constitution

Name

The name of the society is Squamish Civic Forum Society.

Purposes

  • Promote informed, non-partisan civic engagement among residents of Squamish, BC and the Sea to Sky corridor
  • Research, track, and publish information about local government decisions, council voting records, and community issues
  • Facilitate community dialogue and participation in municipal government processes
  • Identify, evaluate, and support candidates for local government who reflect community values and interests
  • Organize community events, forums, and information sessions related to local governance
  • Advocate for transparency, accountability, and evidence-based decision-making in local government
  • Carry out any activities incidental or conducive to the attainment of the above purposes

No Distribution Clause: The society must not, directly or indirectly, return any of its income or assets for the personal benefit of its members. On dissolution, remaining assets must be distributed to one or more organizations with similar purposes.

03
Bylaws Summary

These bylaws are modelled on the BC Societies Act model bylaws. Filing without a lawyer is permitted and common for small societies.

Part 1 — Membership
Open to any individual who supports the purposes. Board may set or waive membership fees. Members in good standing: one vote each at general meetings. Membership terminates on resignation or board resolution for conduct contrary to purposes.
Part 2 — Board of Directors
3–9 directors. Elected by ordinary resolution at each AGM. Board manages all society affairs except matters reserved for members. Quorum = majority of directors in office. Meetings may be held by telephone or video conference.
Part 3 — Officers
Officers: President, Vice-President, Secretary, Treasurer. Elected by the board from directors at first meeting after each AGM. President chairs all meetings and is chief spokesperson. Secretary maintains records and files. Treasurer manages finances and bank accounts.
Part 4 — General Meetings
AGM within 15 months of previous, not more than 6 months after fiscal year end. 14 days notice required (email permitted). Quorum: 10 members or 10% of membership, whichever is less (minimum 3). Virtual meetings permitted.
Part 5 — Finance
Fiscal year ends December 31. Funds deposited in society bank account. Cheques require 2 signing officers. Board may authorize expenditures by ordinary resolution. Expenditures over $1,000 require board approval.
Part 6 — Amendment
Bylaws altered by special resolution (2/3 of votes cast) at a general meeting, provided notice of the alteration was included in the meeting notice.
Part 7 — Dissolution
Dissolved by special resolution of members. After payment of debts, remaining assets distributed to one or more BC organizations with similar purposes, as determined by members.
